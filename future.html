The Future of DNS

Because DNS plays such an important role, in the infrastructure of the Internet, it is unlikely to be going away anytime soon. Given its many vulnerabilities, coupled with the rise in cybercrime, any future enhancements of the DNS protocol are likely to center around strengthening its security. In May of 2024, for example, Microsoft announced a preview of its Zero Trust DNS (ZTDNS), which is planned for release in a future version of Windows (Microsoft). ZTDNS was designed to “support Zero Trust deployments trying to lock down devices to only access approved network destinations” (tojens, 2024). It achieves this by restricting outbound traffic to only those that have been resolved through a Protective DNS (PDNS) service. It integrates the Windows DNS engine with the Windows Firewall, essentially enforcing a zero-trust approach at the DNS level (tojens, 2024).
